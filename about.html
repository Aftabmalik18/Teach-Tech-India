Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) Syllabus Overview



1. Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET)

  • Understand child development and pedagogy principles.
  • Familiarity with primary-level subjects and teaching methods.
  • Assessment of students' learning outcomes and needs.
  • Knowledge of educational policies and practices in India.

2. Teacher Eligibility Test (TET)

  • Focus on curriculum frameworks and teaching strategies.
  • Assessment of teaching skills and classroom management.
  • Understanding of inclusive education and diversity.
  • Knowledge of evaluation techniques and feedback mechanisms.

3. Spoken English Skills

  • Developing effective communication and conversation skills.
  • Understanding pronunciation, intonation, and stress patterns.
  • Engagement in discussions, debates, and presentations.
  • Building vocabulary and enhancing language proficiency.

4. Language Proficiency

  • Grammatical accuracy and usage in spoken contexts.
  • Constructing coherent sentences and paragraphs.
  • Listening comprehension and effective response formulation.
  • Practicing everyday conversations and formal dialogues.

5. Classroom Management Techniques

  • Establishing a positive learning environment.
  • Managing student behavior and promoting engagement.
  • Implementing conflict resolution strategies.
  • Utilizing classroom resources effectively.

6. Assessment and Evaluation

  • Designing formative and summative assessments.
  • Analyzing student performance data for improvement.
  • Providing constructive feedback to students.
  • Understanding assessment tools and techniques.
